3rd Year Roadmap — 5th & 6th Sem

Resume-building and placement preparation focus.

Back to Home View Plan

5th Sem — Resume Building Mode

Focus: Strong projects + intermediate/advanced DSA.

  • DSA/Coding: Revise basics (arrays, strings, linked list, stack, queue, trees). Learn advanced: Graphs, DP, Heaps, Tries. Target 400–500 problems by semester end.
  • Core CS: OS, DBMS, OOP (must for interviews). Start Computer Networks basics.
  • Development: Build 2 strong projects (E‑commerce with cart & auth, Chat app with real-time messaging, or ML project).
  • Career: Start applying for internships (Dec–Jan) and join hackathons / open-source events.

End goal: 2 strong projects on resume, 400+ DSA problems solved, internship applications started.

6th Sem — Placement Prep Begins

  • DSA/Coding: 2–3 problems daily, mock contests (CF/CC). Reach 600+ problems by semester end.
  • Core CS: Revise OS, DBMS, CN, OOP thoroughly. Study system design basics (scalability, APIs, caching).
  • Development: Start major project (final year project): online exam portal, social media clone, or ML app with deployment.
  • Career: Aim for a summer internship after 6th sem. Polish resume, GitHub & LinkedIn.

End goal: Internship offer in hand, 600+ DSA problems solved, major project started.

7th Sem — Placements Full Swing

  • Apply widely for on-campus and off-campus roles.
  • Practice daily coding tests and interviews (mock interviews, timed problems).
  • Build a capstone project (AI/ML, Full-stack, or IoT) that is resume-worthy.
  • Strengthen your online presence (GitHub, LinkedIn, portfolio).

Output: Placement-ready profile and incoming offers.

8th Sem — Final Steps

  • If placed → focus on your final project and wrap up documentation.
  • If not placed → continue applying off-campus and prepare for GATE/GRE/certifications.
  • Secure a plan for either starting your job or pursuing higher studies.

Output: Job offer in hand or a higher-studies plan.

Daily / Weekly Routine

  • Daily: 2 hrs DSA, 1 hr Core CS (OS/DBMS/CN), 1 hr Project work.
  • Weekly: 1 mock test / coding contest, update resume/GitHub, participate in hackathons or clubs.

Why 3rd Year is Crucial

By the end of 6th sem you should be internship-ready. Targets: 500–600 solved problems, 3–4 projects on resume, and at least 1 internship experience by placement season.
